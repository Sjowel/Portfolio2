Moodboard

What did I do?

I made 2 moodboards, the first one based on things I like, like colors, hobbies and destinations.
And the other one was portfolios I liked.

Why did I do that?

I made the first moodboard to see if there was a specific style I liked and wanted to use, that's how I found out I liked pastel colors and wanted to use them for my portfolio.

The second moodboard I looked on WebFlow most of the time, but some of them were from Google images. I put them all together in Figma.

I found out what portfolio style I liked and that I wanted to use light colors with it.

Conclusion

For me it really helped to make a moodboard of stuff I like and to make a moodboard with portfolio designs so I can find designs I liked and make it into my own style.

Typography

What did I do?

I did a font research to find out what font would fit the best with my likings.

Why did I do that?

I wanted a font that will fit me and my style.

How did I do it?

On Figma, I put my name 12 times on a white background and I was scrolling through the fonts and the fonts I liked the most I kept on the white background. When I found 12 fonts I copied some Lorem Ipsum to see what font I prefered in longer text. I got the following feedback on this:
"Don't align your text in the center of the screen but put it left because that's easier readable and never use Lorem Ipsum, if necessary ask chatGPT to write a text for you and use that one."
I implemented this feedback and used my own text on the 5 fonts I liked most.

What were the results?

After doing this research I found out I liked Albert Sans the most. They all kinda look like each other but I thinks the space between de rows is better and it's just not to big.

Conclusion

I think doing a font research is a good way to find out what font you like and want to use for, for example, your portfolio or other designs. It can take a while but I think it's worth it.

Landing page picture

What did I do?

I made 4 designs for my portfolio using my moodboards and font research. I kept in mind that I wanted to use a picture of Maastricht because that is where I am from.

Why did I do that?

I wanted to see what design I liked the most and ask feedback on it to see what other people think about it.

How did I do it?

I made some designs in Figma based on my moodboards. I got the following feedback on it:
"I like the fourth design with in your name the city of Maastricht, but maybe you can keep the picture light in the background and make it move through the letters."
I used the first part from this feedback, to put the picture lighter on the background and I think it turned out great. I got a little more feedback on this design:
"Maybe put a line next to your name to make your name much clearer because it is hard to read at first look right now."
I also did this and it made it much easier to read.
After this I asked feedback once more:
"Maybe just make the background white and the letters a little lower into the buildings so the buildings are jumping out a little more."
I showed him my first design and he agreed what I had now looked better.

What were the results?

The result is what I have now as landing page on my portfolio. It's my name big in the screen with Maastricht light in the background but it is clear that it's Maastricht.

Conclusion

Asking feedback helped me a lot with this design. I had my preferences of course but hearing the opinion of other people helped me to make my design better because I wouldn't come up with some of the ideas.

Color research

What did I do?

My portfolio was still white at this point so I wanted to add more color to it.

Why did I do that?

I thought white was a little boring for what I had in mind for my portfolio so I thought it was a good idea to do a color research.

How did I do it?

I copied the design I had in Figma 6 times to make them all a different color and see what I liked most or if I even like one. I still thought they didn't match with what I had in mind, so I decided to take 2 colors from the picture of Maastricht to use as primary colors for my portfolio, and this worked better.

What were the results?

I found out that I liked the colors from the picture of Maastricht the most and I used them as primary colors for my portfolio. I asked feedback on it:
"It looks like you just took two random colors, try to play a little with the design to see what fits best."
I explained her that I took these color from the picture and I already did a color research. Because I could explain why I choose the colors she found it good that I used them.

Conclusion

When you can tell why you made a decision, people will understand it better and maybe even like it more. I think it's important to know why you made a decision and to be able to explain it to others. I also think it's a good idea to do a color research and match with other colors on your portfolio to see what fits best.
